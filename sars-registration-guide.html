SARS Registration Guide

Complete Step-by-Step Guide to SARS Registration for South African SMMEs
Updated for 2025 | Version 1.0

üìã Overview

This comprehensive guide walks you through the complete SARS registration process, ensuring your business meets all compliance requirements. Registration with SARS is mandatory for all businesses operating in South Africa, and this guide ensures you complete the process correctly the first time.

You have 60 days from business commencement to register with SARS. Failure to register within this timeframe may result in penalties ranging from R250 to R16,000 per month.

üìù Pre-Registration Checklist

‚úÖ Required Documents

Identity Documents: Valid South African ID or passport for all directors/owners
Business Registration: CK1 certificate, founding statement, or partnership agreement
Banking Details: Bank account opened in the business name with account number and branch code
Business Address: Proof of physical business address (lease agreement or utility bill)
Contact Information: Active phone number and email address
Turnover Estimate: Realistic annual turnover projection for the next 12 months

üöÄ Step-by-Step Registration Process

1Choose Registration Method

Option A: Online Registration (Recommended)

  • Visit the SARS eFiling website: www.sarsefiling.co.za
  • Faster processing (3-5 business days)
  • Available 24/7
  • Immediate confirmation of submission

Option B: SARS Branch Registration

  • Book an appointment at your nearest SARS branch
  • Processing time: 7-10 business days
  • Personal assistance available
  • Physical document submission

2Create SARS eFiling Profile

If registering online, you'll need to create an eFiling profile first:

  • Go to www.sarsefiling.co.za and click "Register"
  • Choose "Individual" or "Business" based on your entity type
  • Complete personal information section
  • Set up security questions and password
  • Verify your email address
  • Enable two-factor authentication for security

3Complete IT77 Form

The IT77 form is your primary income tax registration document:

  • Section A: Entity details (business name, type, registration number)
  • Section B: Contact information (address, phone, email)
  • Section C: Banking details (account number, bank name, branch code)
  • Section D: Business activity (main business activities and SIC codes)
  • Section E: Financial information (expected turnover, accounting period)
  • Section F: Director/member details (ID numbers, contact info)

üí° Pro Tip

Double-check all information before submission. Errors can delay processing by weeks and may require starting the process over.

4Determine Additional Tax Types

Based on your business, you may need to register for additional tax types:

VAT Registration (if applicable)

  • Mandatory: If annual turnover exceeds R1 million
  • Voluntary: If turnover is below R1 million but you want to claim VAT
  • Current rate: 15% (confirmed for 2025)

PAYE Registration (if you have employees)

  • Required if you pay salaries to employees
  • Must register before first salary payment
  • Monthly submissions required (EMP201)

Turnover Tax (alternative for small businesses)

  • For businesses with turnover under R1 million
  • Simplified tax calculation (0% to 3% of turnover)
  • Cannot be registered for VAT if using turnover tax

5Submit Registration

Final submission steps:

  • Review all information for accuracy
  • Upload supporting documents (if required)
  • Submit the application
  • Print/save confirmation receipt
  • Note your reference number for tracking

6Await Processing & Follow Up

After submission:

  • Processing time: 3-10 business days
  • Communication: SARS will contact you via email/SMS
  • Tax numbers: You'll receive your tax reference numbers
  • Follow up: Contact SARS if no response after 10 business days

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

Top Registration Pitfalls

  • Incorrect business activity codes: Use the exact SIC codes for your industry
  • Wrong banking details: Ensure account is in the business name, not personal
  • Missing documentation: Have all required documents ready before starting
  • Inconsistent information: Ensure all details match your business registration documents
  • Late registration: Don't wait until the 60-day deadline approaches
  • Multiple registrations: Don't register the same entity multiple times
