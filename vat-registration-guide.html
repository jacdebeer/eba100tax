üìä VAT Overview

Value-Added Tax (VAT) is a consumption tax levied on most goods and services in South Africa. Understanding when and how to register for VAT is crucial for business compliance and cash flow management.

‚ö†Ô∏è 2025 VAT Rate Update

Current VAT Rate: 15% (confirmed stable for 2025)

Previous discussions about increases to 15.5% or 16% have been postponed. The rate remains at 15% throughout 2025.

üìè VAT Registration Thresholds

üí∞ Mandatory Registration Threshold

R1,000,000 per year

  • Based on turnover in any consecutive 12-month period
  • Must register within 30 days of exceeding threshold
  • Applies to taxable supplies only (excluding exempt supplies)
  • Includes anticipated turnover for new businesses

üí° Voluntary Registration

You can register for VAT even if below the R1 million threshold if:

  • You want to claim VAT on business purchases
  • Your business credibility requires VAT registration
  • You supply mainly to VAT-registered businesses
  • You want to improve cash flow through VAT refunds

üöÄ Step-by-Step Registration Process

1Determine Registration Necessity

Calculate Your Turnover

  • Review last 12 months of sales (for existing businesses)
  • Project next 12 months turnover (for new businesses)
  • Include only taxable supplies in calculation
  • Exclude exempt supplies (financial services, residential rentals, etc.)

Consider Business Benefits

  • Input VAT claims: Claim VAT on business purchases
  • Cash flow: Potential monthly VAT refunds
  • Business credibility: Professional appearance to clients
  • Compliance cost: Additional admin and accounting requirements

2Complete VAT Registration Application

VAT registration is completed through your existing SARS eFiling profile:

  • Log into SARS eFiling
  • Navigate to "Work Online" ‚Üí "Register for Tax Type"
  • Select "Value-Added Tax (VAT)"
  • Complete the VAT101 form online

3Provide Registration Details

Business Information

  • Legal business name and trading names
  • Primary business activity and SIC codes
  • Expected monthly/annual turnover
  • Anticipated VAT liability or refund position

Operational Details

  • Business premises address and contact details
  • Accounting period end date
  • Preferred VAT return frequency (monthly/bi-monthly)
  • Banking details for VAT refunds

4Submit Supporting Documents

Upload the following documents through eFiling:

  • Bank confirmation letter on bank letterhead
  • Proof of business address (lease agreement/utility bill)
  • Financial statements or turnover projections
  • Recent invoices showing business activity
  • Professional registration certificates (if applicable)

üí° Input VAT Claims

You can claim VAT on business purchases if:

  • You have a valid VAT invoice
  • Purchase is for business purposes
  • Supplier is VAT-registered
  • VAT invoice shows your business details correctly

VAT Invoice Requirements

  • Word "TAX INVOICE" clearly displayed
  • Supplier's VAT number
  • Customer's name and VAT number
  • Invoice date and number
  • Description of goods/services
  • VAT amount or "VAT inclusive" statement

üö® Common VAT Mistakes

Registration Issues

  • Late registration: Not registering within 30 days of threshold
  • Incorrect threshold calculation: Including exempt supplies
  • Missing documentation: Incomplete registration applications

Return Filing Errors

  • Late returns: Missing the 25th deadline
  • Incorrect calculations: VAT amount errors
  • Missing invoices: Claiming VAT without proper documentation
  • Wrong periods: Including transactions in wrong VAT periods

Record Keeping Problems

  • Poor filing systems: Can't locate supporting documents
  • Missing VAT invoices: Can't claim input VAT
  • Inadequate controls: No VAT reconciliation processes

üîç VAT Deregistration

1When to Deregister

  • Turnover below threshold: Annual turnover drops below R1 million
  • Business cessation: Business stops trading
  • Activity change: Switch to exempt supplies only
  • Voluntary decision: No longer beneficial to be VAT registered

2Deregistration Process

  • Apply through SARS eFiling using VAT102 form
  • Submit final VAT return for partial period
  • Account for output VAT on remaining stock
  • Repay any outstanding VAT refunds received
  • Keep records for 5 years after deregistration
