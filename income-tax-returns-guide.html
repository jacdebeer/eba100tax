üéØ Auto-Assessment System

1How Auto-Assessment Works

SARS automatically calculates your tax based on information they already have:

  • IRP5 certificates: From your employer(s)
  • IT3(a) certificates: Investment income from banks
  • Medical aid certificates: Medical scheme contributions
  • Previous year information: Deductions and allowances

2Auto-Assessment Eligibility

You qualify for auto-assessment if:

  • Your income is mainly from employment (IRP5)
  • You receive investment income below R30,000
  • You don't have significant changes from previous year
  • No new deductions or allowances to claim
  • SARS has all your necessary certificates

üí° Auto-Assessment Benefits

  • No filing required: SARS calculates everything automatically
  • Faster refunds: Processed within 72 hours if refund due
  • Reduced errors: Based on verified third-party data
  • SMS/Email notification: Receive assessment notification

üìã Return Types and Requirements

1ITR12 - Individual Return

Who must file:

  • Individuals earning above R500,000 per year
  • Those with multiple income sources
  • Business owners and sole proprietors
  • Rental property owners
  • Anyone wanting to claim additional deductions

Key sections to complete:

  • Employment income: Salary, wages, bonuses
  • Business income: Trading income and expenses
  • Investment income: Interest, dividends, rental
  • Deductions: Medical expenses, donations, retirement contributions
  • Tax credits: Medical scheme fees, donations

2ITR14 - Trust Return

Trust filing requirements:

  • All trusts must file annual returns
  • Due date: January 31, 2026
  • Include trust income, distributions, and beneficiary details
  • Attach supporting schedules for complex trusts

3Company Income Tax Returns

Company return requirements:

  • All companies must file within 12 months of year-end
  • Include provisional tax payments and estimates
  • Submit audited financial statements if required
  • Calculate normal tax and other company taxes

üöó Travel and Motor Vehicle Deductions

1Travel Allowance vs Reimbursement

Travel Allowance (included in salary)

  • Fixed monthly amount for travel
  • Included in taxable income
  • Can claim actual expenses with logbook
  • Alternative: Use prescribed rates per kilometer

Travel Reimbursement (actual costs)

  • Employer reimburses actual travel costs
  • Not included in taxable income
  • No additional deductions available
  • Must provide receipts to employer

2Logbook Requirements

What to record for each business trip:

  • Date: Date of travel
  • Odometer readings: Start and end readings
  • Destination: Where you traveled to
  • Purpose: Business reason for travel
  • Distance: Total kilometers traveled

Logbook maintenance period:

  • Keep detailed records for full 12-month period
  • Calculate business use percentage
  • Apply percentage to vehicle expenses
  • Update every 3 years or when usage changes significantly
