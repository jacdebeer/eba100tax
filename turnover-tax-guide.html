Turnover Tax for SMMEs

Complete Guide to the Simplified Tax Option for Small and Medium Enterprises
Updated for 2025 | Version 1.0

üìä What is Turnover Tax?

Turnover Tax is a simplified tax option designed specifically for small and medium enterprises (SMMEs) in South Africa. It replaces normal income tax, provisional tax, and CGT for qualifying businesses, offering a streamlined approach to tax compliance.

üí° Key Benefits of Turnover Tax

  • Simplified calculations: Tax based on turnover, not profit
  • One annual return: No monthly or provisional tax payments
  • Lower compliance costs: Reduced accounting and admin requirements
  • Progressive rates: 0% to 3% based on turnover levels
  • Easy exit: Can opt out if business grows beyond threshold

‚úÖ Eligibility Requirements

1Turnover Threshold

Annual turnover must not exceed R1 million

  • Based on receipts and accruals for the tax year
  • Includes all business income from all sources
  • Excludes exempt income (e.g., interest from bank deposits)
  • Calculated on cash or accrual basis (must be consistent)

2Business Type Requirements

Eligible businesses include:

  • Sole proprietorships
  • Companies (including close corporations)
  • Trusts conducting business
  • Co-operatives

Excluded businesses:

  • Personal service providers with qualification requirements
  • Businesses with more than 20% investment income
  • Mining and petroleum operations
  • Businesses with more than 20% rental income

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Restrictions

  • Cannot register for VAT: Turnover tax and VAT are mutually exclusive
  • No input deductions: Cannot claim business expenses
  • Annual assessment: Tax calculated on total annual turnover
  • Exit threshold: Must exit if turnover exceeds R1 million

üí∞ Tax Rate Structure

üìä 2025 Turnover Tax Rates

Annual Turnover Tax Rate Tax Amount
R0 - R335,000 0% R0
R335,001 - R500,000 1% 1% of amount above R335,000
R500,001 - R750,000 2% R1,650 + 2% of amount above R500,000
R750,001 - R1,000,000 3% R6,650 + 3% of amount above R750,000

1Tax Calculation Examples

Example 1: Annual turnover R400,000

  • Tax-free portion: R335,000 (0% tax)
  • Taxable portion: R400,000 - R335,000 = R65,000
  • Tax calculation: R65,000 √ó 1% = R650
  • Total tax payable: R650

Example 2: Annual turnover R600,000

  • First R335,000: R0 (0% rate)
  • Next R165,000 (R335,001-R500,000): R165,000 √ó 1% = R1,650
  • Remaining R100,000 (R500,001-R600,000): R100,000 √ó 2% = R2,000
  • Total tax payable: R0 + R1,650 + R2,000 = R3,650

Example 3: Annual turnover R900,000

  • First R335,000: R0
  • Next R165,000: R1,650 (1% rate)
  • Next R250,000: R5,000 (2% rate)
  • Remaining R150,000: R4,500 (3% rate)
  • Total tax payable: R11,150

üìù Application Process (TT01)

1When to Apply

  • New businesses: Within 60 days of commencement
  • Existing businesses: Before the start of the tax year you want to use turnover tax
  • Change from normal tax: Must apply before new tax year begins
  • Annual decision: Can change election annually

2TT01 Application Form

Complete the TT01 form through SARS eFiling:

  • Business details: Registration numbers, contact information
  • Activity description: Nature of business operations
  • Turnover estimate: Expected annual turnover
  • Previous tax status: Current tax registrations
  • Declaration: Confirm eligibility and understanding

3Supporting Documentation

  • Business registration documents
  • Financial statements or projections
  • Bank statements showing business activity
  • Confirmation of business activities

üìä Annual Return Filing (TT03)

1TT03 Return Requirements

Filing deadline: Within 6 months of tax year-end

  • Individual/CC: By August 31 (February year-end)
  • Companies: Within 6 months of financial year-end
  • Electronic filing: Must be submitted through eFiling

2Information Required for TT03

  • Total turnover: All business receipts for the year
  • Exempt income: Interest and other exempt receipts
  • Tax calculation: Apply progressive rates to taxable turnover
  • Payments made: Any provisional or other tax payments
  • Banking details: For refunds or additional payments

‚úÖ Record Keeping Requirements

Income records: All sales invoices, receipts, cash register tapes
Bank statements: All business bank account statements
Cash records: Daily cash takings and deposit records
Customer invoices: All invoices issued to customers
Contract records: Agreements and service contracts
Monthly summaries: Monthly turnover summaries

üîÑ Switching Between Tax Systems

1Exiting Turnover Tax

Mandatory exit situations:

  • Annual turnover exceeds R1 million
  • Business activities change to excluded categories
  • Investment income exceeds 20% of total income
  • Want to register for VAT

Voluntary exit:

  • Can choose to exit at any year-end
  • Submit notification before new tax year
  • Cannot re-enter for 5 years after voluntary exit

2Transition to Normal Tax

  • Register for normal income tax
  • Set up provisional tax payments
  • Implement proper accounting systems
  • Consider VAT registration if turnover warrants
  • File final TT03 return for last turnover tax year

üí° When to Choose Turnover Tax

Turnover tax is beneficial when:

  • Your profit margin is high (above 10-15%)
  • You have minimal business expenses
  • You want simplified compliance
  • Your turnover is well below R1 million
  • You don't need VAT registration

Normal tax is better when:

  • You have high business expenses
  • Your profit margin is low
  • You need VAT registration
  • You want to claim all business deductions
  • You have complex business structures

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Pitfalls and Penalties

üö® Avoid These Mistakes

Application Errors

  • Late application: Missing the 60-day deadline for new businesses
  • Incorrect eligibility: Applying when not eligible
  • Missing supporting documents: Incomplete applications

Compliance Issues

  • Late returns: Missing the 6-month filing deadline
  • Incorrect turnover: Understating or miscalculating turnover
  • Poor record keeping: Inadequate supporting documentation

Exit Timing

  • Exceeding threshold: Not exiting when turnover exceeds R1 million
  • Wrong tax year: Confusion about when changes take effect
  • VAT registration: Registering for VAT while on turnover tax
